Overview

Intersectionality is not a new topic but due to a deeper attention to social justice, it has become a more discussed topic in society. It has even infiltrated the discussion in the political arena. This week's topic we explore the definition of intersectionality, brief view it's roots in the legal system, and how it impacts the day to day work to seek a more diverse and inclusive society. There is even a National Academies Committee using intersectionality as the lens to explore the underrepresentation of women of color in tech fields. Intersectionality is here to stay.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you should be able to:

  1. Define the concept of intersectionality.
  2. Provide examples about how intersectionality is not about victim-hood or identity politics.
  3. Explain why ignoring intersectionality presents a challenge to any efforts seeking social justice and inclusivity in our modern organizations.
