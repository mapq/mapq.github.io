Students Supervised

I have worked with many students over my years in academia. As of this update, I have supervised (or co-supervised) 10 Phd students. I have also supervised many MS students and served on many more committees. This page has a list of the students I supervised, with a link to their dissertation/thesis and in some cases, a link to their current employment or website. I have also worked with lots of undergraduate students, their information will be included at a later date.

Phd Dissertations Supervised

As of this writing, all of these dissertations were from the Department of Computer Science, Virginia Tech. For all of them I have included a link to their dissertation, in case you need some bedtime reading. Where possible, I have included a link to their current website and additional info.

  1. Yao Wang, Exploring the Role of Prospective Memory in Location-Based Reminders, 2017-05-03. http://hdl.handle.net/10919/77583. Employer: MobileIron.
  2. Blake Sawyer, The Physical-Social Context in Information Refinding, 2015-05-05. http://hdl.handle.net/10919/70919.
  3. Ben Hanrahan, Getting Lost in Email: How and Why Users Spend More Time in Email than Intended, 2014-12-04. http://hdl.handle.net/10919/51204. Employer: Assistant Professor, College of Information Sciences and Technology, Penn State. ACM Pubs Page, Google Scholar.
  4. Ashley Robinson, The Attitudes of African American Middle School Girls Toward Computer Science: Influences of Home, School, and Technology Use, 2015-05-13. Co-Advisor Dr. Glenda Scales. http://hdl.handle.net/10919/52277. Employer: Intel, Arizona.
  5. Turner, Scott Alexander, Peer Review in CS2: the Effects on Attitudes, Engagement, and Conceptual Learning, 2009-08-13. http://hdl.handle.net/10919/28813. Employer: Assistant Professor, Department of Mathematics, Statistics & Computer Science, University of Wisconsin, Stout. ACM Pubs Page.
  6. Tungare, Manas, Mental Workload in Personal Information Management: Understanding PIM Practices Across Multiple Devices, 2009-03-25. http://hdl.handle.net/10919/26878. Employer: Engineering Leader, Search Platforms at Google.
  7. Capra, Robert G., An Investigation of Finding and Refinding Information on the Web, 2006-02-16. http://hdl.handle.net/10919/26342. Employer: Associate Professor at School of Information and Library Science, University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. ACM Pubs Page, Google Scholar. NSF CAREER awardee.
  8. Rode, Jochen, Web Application Development by Nonprogrammers: User-Centered Design of an End-User Web Development Tool, 2005-07-01. Co-Advisor Dr. Mary Beth Rosson, http://hdl.handle.net/10919/28222. Employer: SAP, Germany.
  9. Ali, Mir Farooq, A Transformation-based Approach to Building Multi-Platform User Interfaces Using a Task Model and the User Interface Markup Language, 2004-11-16. http://hdl.handle.net/10919/27753. Employer: Engineer at Motorola.
  10. Lewis, Tracy L., Design Readiness:An Exploratory Model of Object-Oriented Design Performance, 2004-07-16. http://hdl.handle.net/10919/28545. Co-Advisor Dr. Mary Beth Rosson. Employer: Faculty Associate, Computer Science, iSchool at University of Winsconsin, Madison. ACM Pubs Page.
